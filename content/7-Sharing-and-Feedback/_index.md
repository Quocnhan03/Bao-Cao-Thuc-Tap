## Overall Evaluation

### 1. Working Environment
The working environment at FCJ is very friendly, open, and professional. Everyone, from mentors to interns, is always ready to support each other, even outside of working hours. Over the 12 weeks, I had the opportunity to practice various AWS knowledge domains — from designing VPC network architectures, managing EC2/RDS, deploying ECS Clusters, CI/CD Pipelines, configuring WAF, AWS Systems Manager, to building the capstone Smart Parking IoT project integrated with IoT Core, Lambda, Rekognition, and Bedrock. Whenever I encountered issues with AWS configurations or ESP32 hardware connections, I always received quick feedback from the team. The workspace is neat and comfortable, helping me focus better on weekly practical exercises.

### 2. Support from Mentors / Team Admins
Mentors provided very detailed and patient guidance, especially during complex labs such as CI/CD Pipeline deployment, WAF configuration, or Systems Manager setup. What I appreciated most is that mentors always encouraged me to research and debug on my own before giving hints, helping me hone my practical problem-solving mindset. Team admins were highly supportive regarding administrative procedures, documentation, and creating favorable conditions for me to complete the 12-week program on schedule.

### 3. Relevance to My Major
Since I major in Applied Machine Learning, IoT and AI concepts like Amazon Rekognition and Bedrock were not entirely new to me, as I had studied the fundamentals at university. Regarding AWS, I had previously used some basic services but hadn't gone deep. The FCJ program helped me systematically consolidate my AWS knowledge — from network infrastructure (VPC, Subnets, Route Tables) to system administration (EC2, RDS, ECS) and Serverless architectures. Notably, the Smart Parking IoT capstone project allowed me to combine what I learned in school (IoT, AI) with what I newly learned at FCJ (Lambda, API Gateway, DynamoDB) into a complete product.

### 4. Opportunities for Learning & Skill Development
Although I already had a foundation in IoT and AI from university, the FCJ program elevated my skills to a new level: knowing how to practically deploy them on Cloud infrastructure. Over the 12 weeks, I learned how to systematically design Cloud architectures, connect ESP32 hardware with AWS IoT Core, build Serverless Backends using Lambda/API Gateway, write technical reports using Hugo Workshop, and manage source code on GitHub. Mentors also shared a lot of practical experience, giving me a clearer perspective on the Cloud Engineer career path.

### 5. Culture & Team Spirit
The culture at FCJ is highly positive: everyone respects each other, works seriously, yet maintains a fun atmosphere. When facing the capstone project deadline, the entire team reviewed, gave feedback, and supported each other to refine the product. This made me feel like a true part of the collective, despite being just an intern. The "go far together" spirit of FCJ left a strong impression on me.

### 6. Policies / Benefits for Interns
The company provides internship allowances and flexible working hours when necessary. Furthermore, being provided with a dedicated AWS account for practice, access to internal documents, and participation in in-depth training sessions are massive advantages compared to other internship programs.

## Other Questions

**What were you most satisfied with during your internship?**
Building a complete IoT system from scratch, starting with hardware (ESP32 Camera, ultrasonic sensors) to software (AWS Lambda, API Gateway, DynamoDB, Rekognition), and deploying it for real on AWS. The feeling when the system correctly recognized a license plate and automatically opened the gate for the first time is my most memorable moment of the internship.

**What do you think the company should improve for future interns?**
Adding more experience-sharing workshops among different intern cohorts (alumni sharing) would be great so that newcomers can learn from the experiences of predecessors. Also, providing guidance on the Agile/Scrum workflow right from the first week would help interns adapt to the work organization much faster.

**If you were to recommend this to friends, would you advise them to intern here? Why?**
Absolutely yes. The FCJ program at AWS Vietnam is one of the best IT internship programs I've known for students. You not only learn theory but also get to practice on real AWS infrastructure, build practical projects, and receive dedicated guidance from mentors. This is a rare opportunity to step into the Cloud Computing field methodically.

## Suggestions & Desires

**Do you have any suggestions to improve the internship experience?**
I suggest the FCJ team organize more mid-term "Demo Days" for interns to present their project progress, receive early feedback, and adjust their direction timely. Additionally, a shared Q&A channel (e.g., a Slack channel or Discord) for interns to quickly discuss technical issues would be very helpful.

**Would you like to continue with this program in the future?**
Yes, I strongly desire to participate in advanced programs or become a mentor/teaching assistant for future FCJ cohorts. The experience from the 12-week internship has given me a solid foundation, and I want to give back to the community.

**Other feedback:**
Thank you to the FCJ team and AWS Vietnam for creating such an incredibly high-quality internship program. The 12-week experience here not only helped me improve my professional skills but also transformed how I think about building systems on the Cloud. I believe that the knowledge and experience gained will be valuable assets for my future career.
